About

Linda Sen grew up on a large farm on the great plains of North Dakota. Eleventh in a family of 13 children, she did her undergraduate work at University of North Dakota and earned a Masters Degree in Fine Arts at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago. In 1970, she married Siddhartha Sen from Calcutta and two years later moved to Woodridge, IL where she still resides.

Linda and her husband raised 3 fine boys: Shilad, Shourin and Sunil. In 1997, her husband and best friend died. He had always encouraged her to pursue her artwork and built a studio for her in their home.

Linda continued her artwork while rasing her family, and in 1988 she started a commercial art business specializing in murals and specialty faux finishes including gold leafing and Venetian plaster. During this time, she has continued her personal art journey with pastels, watercolor and oil mediums. Linda's artwork uses layered techniques, with a strong sense of color and design.
